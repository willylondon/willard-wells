Just as you are what you eat, your mind is shaped by what it consumes. We are increasingly aware of the need for healthy physical diets, yet we gorge ourselves on hollow information calories all day long.

Doomscrolling the news, mindlessly swiping through short-form video feeds, arguing with strangers on the internet. It's the equivalent of eating fast food for breakfast, lunch, and dinner.

I've been trying to curate my information diet more intentionally. 

- Less breaking news, more long-form essays and books.
- Less reaction, more reflection.
- Unfollowing algorithmic feeds in favor of chronological RSS readers.

The goal isn't to be ignorant of the world, but to engage with it in a way that is constructive rather than debilitating. A digital garden like this one is an attempt to create a slow, intentional space for thought amidst the noise.
