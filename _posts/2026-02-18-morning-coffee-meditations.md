My morning coffee routine has become sacrosanct. It is no longer just a mechanism for caffeine delivery; it's a ritual that grounds me before the day chaos begins.

I use a manual pour-over method. The process requires attention. Measuring the beans, waiting for the water to reach the precise temperature, the slow, circular pour over the grounds. If you rush it, the coffee tells you. It demands patience.

In a world that constantly begs for speed and efficiency, taking 10 minutes to do one thing slowly is an act of rebellion. It sets the tone for the rest of the day.

When I finally sit down with the warm mug, I try not to look at my phone. Just me, the coffee, and the quiet of the morning. It's the best part of the day.
